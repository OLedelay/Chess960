Chess960 Rules

Chess960 is a chess variant created by Bobby Fischer (considered to be the greatest chess player of all time). Chess960 is just like regular chess, except that the starting lineup between your 2 rooks is shuffled in a random order every time you start a new game. The purpose of this chess variant was to make memorzing chess openings far less useful, forcing the player to rely on his/her intuition.

The starting position for Chess960 must meet the following requirments:
  • Pawns are placed on their orthodox home squares.
  • All remaining pieces are placed on the first rank.
  • The king must be placed somewhere between the 2 white rooks.
  • The bishops are placed on opposite coloured squares.
  • The black pieces are placed equal-and-opposite to the white pieces. For example, if white's king is placed on b1, then black's king is placed on b8.
